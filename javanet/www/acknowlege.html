Free, simple, powerful: Transparent Distributed Computing
Some folks who are making a big difference
  • Richard M. Stallman and the Free Software Foundation, for starting it all, and for donating the cajo namespace. If you don't know who RMS is, you should definitely read this excellent book about him! OK, well, technically; I have only requested the namespace. Oh yes, and publicly: I apologise Richard, for venting at MIT. It's just that I passionately believe that Java is better than anything out there now, and I know it will be free soon!

  • Eric S. Raymond, and also Rob Landley, with whom I had the pleasure to confer with at length, at Penguicon. For encouraging me to open my most precious and personal source code; you made the case guys, I did it! By the way, congratulations Eric, on your latest excellent book!

  • Chris DiBona, your advice and encouragement meant a lot to me at the start of this adventure. Your book was also a pivotal inspiration.

  • Esmond Pitt (aka) ejp on the Sun Java RMI forum; for patiently answering my, and many others questions over the years, especially as they became more esoteric. Also for your outstanding book, on my very favourite subject no less!

  • Project member Fredrik Larsen, who with assistance from project member Li Ma, 'changed the rules' of Java Reflection. Now, for the project's invoke paradigm: Subclasses and subinterfaces will be recognised. Great work guys!

  • Project member Ekkehard Gentz; who demonstrated that the example needed a root properties file, to work on non-English localised machines. He also provided the example properties file translated auf Deutsch!

  • Fellow free software developer Jean-Marie Dautelle; for your experienced feedback to this project, and your generous contribution of an Ant script, to build the framework. Jean-Marie heads two excellent free software projects. They provide classes and packages missing from core Java; for real-time, scientific, and safety-critical applications. I highly recommend a visit to learn more about both: JScience and javolution. He even provided the example properties file translated en français!

  • Olivier Brizard, for your proposal to include support for HTTP proxy tunnels, as required by so many corporations. You both made the case, and validated its operation. You are helping to foster a new era of cooperation, between fiercely firewalled corporations: Détente.

  • More to come!
    • A special thanks to the entire community... Together Everyone Achieves More!
