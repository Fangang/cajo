About the project license
The cajo project source code is licensed under the GNU LGPL. The stultifying lawyerese can be found here. The short, human-readable preamble is definitely worth a look.

Allow me to summarise what I feel are the two most important bits:
  • This license grants you the right to use this source code in your applications, without payment of any royalty to the copyright holder; he being I.

  • In a feature not present in the regular GPL; this license grants you the right to use this library in your proprietary applications, without having to make the combined code free.
Many will argue that this second point makes the license less free than the GPL. However, I believe this framework has its greatest opportunity to benefit application development, if it is a resource for all developers, not just the free ones. Just as with its objects; this framework allows all of its developers to work together: Whether they have the option to make their code free, or not.

However, this license does impose the copyleft provision; which in order to use this code, you must also agree:
If you modify the gnu.cajo classes, its interfaces, or its packages; or if you add classes or interfaces to its packages, or create subpackages: The resulting packages must also be licensed under the terms of the GNU LGPL.
Note: extending the classes, and implementing the interfaces, in your own packages, is not subject to this provision.
