About the project licensing...

 This framework is licensed under the GNU LGPL, the technical lawyerese can be found here. The short, human-readable preamble, is definitely worth reading.

Let me summarise the two most important parts:
  • This license grants you the right to use this code in your applications, without payment of any royalties to the copyright holder; he being I.

  • In a feature not present in the regular GPL; you have the right to use this library in proprietary applications, without having to make the combined code free.
Many will argue that this second point makes the license "less free" than the GPL. However, I believe this framework has its greatest opportunity to influence application development if it is a resource for all developers, not just the free ones. Just as with its objects, this framework allows all of its developers to work together; whether they have the option to make their code free or not.

The license does impose an important provision however; which in order to use this code, you must also agree:
If you modify the gnu.cajo classes, its interfaces, or its packages; if you add classes or interfaces to its packages, or create subpackages: The resulting packages if sold, or given away, must also be licensed under the terms of the GNU LGPL. Note: extending the classes and implementing the interfaces in your packages, are not subject to this provision.

   Finally, you are both welcomed, and encouraged to identify your application's distributed capability by using this button; on webpages, in documentation, and for products. Of course it is purely optional, and also free. On a webpage, a tag could be as simple as this:
<a href=https://cajo.dev.java.net/index.html>
