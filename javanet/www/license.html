About the project licensing...

 This framework is licensed under the GNU LGPL, the technical legalese can be found here. The short, human-readable preamble is definitely worth reading.

Let me summarise the two most important parts:
  • This license grants you the right to use this code in your applications, without payment of any royalties to the copyright holder; he being me.

  • In a feature not present in the regular GPL; you have the right to use this library in proprietary applications, without having to make the combined code free.
Now, many will argue that this second point makes the license "less free" than the GPL, in fact I also agree that it does. However, this framework has its greatest opportunity to influence application development, if it is a resource for all developers, not just the free ones. Just as with its objects; this framework allows its developers to work together, whether they have the option to make their code free or not.

The license does impose one important provision however; which in order to use this code, you must also abide:
If you modify or extend the gnu.cajo classes, or rename them, or their packages; both the resulting classes and packages must also be licensed under the terms of the GNU LGPL.
This requirement is simply to keep this code, and all its future enhancements, free for everyone; to use, to extend, and to share.
