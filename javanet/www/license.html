About the package licensing.

 This package is licensed under the GNU LGPL, the technical legalese can be found here. I will try to summarize it. The license grants you the right to use this code, as you wish, without payment of any royalties to the author, he being me. Further, in a feature not present in the regular GPL: you can use this library in a proprietary application, without having to make the resulting code free.

Some people will argue that this makes the license "less free" than the GPL, and perhaps it is. However I don't believe library software can really be free when its license also tells you how you can't use it. This framework has its greatest chance to influence application development if it is a resource for all developers, not just the free ones. Just as with its objects, this framework lets all of its developers work together too; whether they choose to make their code free or not.

Don't get me wrong, I love the GPL. I think it is the perfect license for applications, especially operating systems. For libraries however, I wish rms would have left the original acronym, meaning "Library General Public License", rather than changing it to the "Lesser General Public License". I think the LGPL should have been strictly limited to only cover libraries, and specifically excluding applications.

Final thought: I would most apprecitate if you distributed the framework source with your applications, or at least a link back to this site. But to allow you ultimate freedom; I'll even leave that up to you.
