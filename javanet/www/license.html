About the project license
The cajo project source code is licensed to you under the terms of the GNU LGPL. The stultifying lawyerese can be found here. The short human-readable preamble, is certainly worth a look.

Allow me to summarise what I feel are the four most important bits:
  • This license grants you the right to use this source code, for any purpose.

  • This license grants you the right to study and modify this code, to meet your needs.

  • This license grants you the right to distribute this code, under this same license.

  • This license grants you the right to distribute your application that uses this code, under any license of your choosing.
Many will say this last point makes the license less free than the GPL. However, just as with its objects, this framework allows its developers to work together; whether they have the option to make their code free, or not.

This license imposes the very important Copyleft provision; which if you wish to distribute this code, you must also accept:
If you modify the project classes, its interfaces, or its packages; if you add classes or interfaces to its packages, or if you create subpackages:
The resulting code can only be distributed under the terms of this license.
However, this license permits extending these classes, and implementing these interfaces, in your own packages, without being subject to Copyleft.
