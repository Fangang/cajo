About the project license
The cajo project source code is licensed under the GNU LGPL. The stultifying lawyerese can be found here. The short human-readable preamble is definitely worth a look.

Allow me to summarise what I feel are the three most important bits:
  • This license grants you the right to use this source code in your applications, for any purpose.

  • This license grants you the freedom to study, and to modify this code, to meet your needs.

  • This license grants you the right to distribute this, or your modified code, with your applications; without having to release your code under these terms.
Many will object that this last point makes the license less free than the GPL. However, just as with its objects, this framework allows all of its developers to work together; whether they have the option to make their code free, or not.

This license does impose the copyleft provision however, which in order to distribute any of this code, you must also accept:
If you modify the gnu.cajo classes, its interfaces, or its packages; if you add classes or interfaces to its packages, or if you create subpackages:
The resulting packages must also be distributed under the terms of the GNU LGPL. (or the GNU GPL, if you prefer)
Note: extending the classes, and implementing the interfaces, in your own packages, is not subject to this provision.
